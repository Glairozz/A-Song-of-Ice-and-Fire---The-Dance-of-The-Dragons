Dragons that ruled Westeros

Dragons in the world of Westeros trace their origins back to the ancient Valyrian Freehold, where they were first tamed and bred into living weapons. Giants like Balerion the Black Dread, Meraxes, and Vhagar helped carve out the Valyrian empire and later enabled Aegon the Conqueror to forge the Seven Kingdoms through fire and blood. These early dragons were immense, terrifying creatures—symbols of dominion whose legacy set the foundation for centuries of Targaryen power.

During the Dance of the Dragons, the last great age of dragonkind, more than twenty dragons still lived, each with unique temperaments, skills, and bonds to their riders. The skies of Westeros teemed with iconic beasts like the crimson Caraxes, the enormous and ancient Vhagar, the golden Sunfyre, and the bronze Vermithor. Carefully raised in Dragonstone and the Dragonpit, these dragons served not only as engines of war but also as political instruments—each one shaping succession, loyalty, and legitimacy during the brutal Targaryen civil war.

By the era of A Song of Ice and Fire, dragons have all but vanished from the world. Only three survive—Drogon, Rhaegal, and Viserion—hatched by Daenerys Targaryen centuries after the last known dragon died. Unlike their ancestors, these dragons grow without the guidance of dragonkeepers, established breeding practices, or the vast Valyrian knowledge once used to control their kind. Their return marks a resurgence of magic and a reminder of the Targaryens’ lost greatness, providing a stark contrast to the disciplined, widespread power dragons once held during the height of their reign.

Dragons from the Ancestor Era

Balerion the Black Dread

The largest and most feared Targaryen dragon; black scales, black fire, and wings that blocked out the sun. Ridden by Aegon the Conqueror.

Vhagar

A titan nearly as large as Balerion; bronze and green; lived for over 170 years. Ridden by Visenya, then Laena, then Aemond.

Meraxes

Massive silver dragon with golden eyes; ridden by Rhaenys Targaryen. Fell during the Dornish Wars.

Vermithor (“Bronze Fury”)

Huge bronze dragon once ridden by King Jaehaerys I; one of the largest dragons ever after Balerion and Vhagar.

Silverwing

A gentle silver dragon ridden by Queen Alysanne; known for her calm temperament.

Dance of The Dragon Era

Caraxes (“Blood Wyrm”)

Long, serpentine, blood-red dragon with a piercing scream; ridden by Daemon Targaryen.

Syrax

Yellow-gold dragon ridden by Rhaenyra Targaryen; fast and aggressive, raised mostly in captivity.

Sunfyre (“the Golden”)

Brilliant golden scales; considered the most beautiful dragon in history; ridden by Aegon II.

Meleys (“Red Queen”)

Red and pink scaled, extremely fast; ridden by Rhaenys Targaryen.

Moondancer

Small but incredibly quick green dragon ridden by Baela Targaryen; built for agility rather than size.

Seasmoke

Grey-white dragon, nimble and responsive; ridden first by Laenor Velaryon (show) and later Addam of Hull.

Vermax

Fiery young dragon ridden by Jacaerys Velaryon; known for rapid growth.

Arrax

White-and-gold young dragon ridden by Lucerys Velaryon; quick but inexperienced.

Tessarion (“Blue Queen”)

Shimmering cobalt dragon with copper flames; graceful and mid-sized.

Tyraxes

Small, still-growing dragon bonded to Joffrey Velaryon; not battle-tested.

Sheepstealer

Wild brown dragon known for stealing livestock; eventually bonded to Ulf White.

Cannibal

Untamed black dragon of Dragonstone who fed on dead dragons and hatchlings; never ridden.

Grey Ghost

Wild pale-grey dragon that avoided humans; rarely seen.

A Song of Ice and Fire Era

Drogon

Black-and-red, largest of Daenerys’s dragons; fierce, intelligent, and the closest in spirit to Balerion.

Rhaegal

Green-and-bronze dragon; aggressive but loyal; named after Rhaegar Targaryen.

Viserion

Cream-and-gold dragon; fiery and swift; becomes an undead ice dragon in the show.
